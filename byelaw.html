Part - I

INTRODUCTION

1.1. These Bye-laws may be called the "Bye-Laws of Kerala School of Mathematics". These shall come into effect from the date of approval by the Government.

1.2. These Bye-laws may be read in conjunction with the Rules of Kerala School of Mathematics.

2. Name and address of the Institution

2.1. The name of the institute shall be Kerala School of Mathematics, abbreviated as KSoM.

2.2. The registered office of the institute shall be

Kerala School of Mathematics
Kunnamangalam
Kozhikode - 673571
Kerala, India

2.3. KSoM shall function within the territory of India.

3. Definitions

3.1. In these Bye-laws, the following words and abbreviations shall have the meanings given against them, unless the context signifies otherwise:

  • "Act" means Travancore-Cochin, Literacy, Scientific & Charitable Societies Registration Act, 1955.
  • "Institute" means Kerala School of Mathematics.
  • "Government" means Government of Kerala.
  • "Central Government" means the Government of India represented by the Department of Atomic Energy.
  • "NBHM" means the National Board for Higher Mathematics.
  • "Governing Body" means the Governing Body of KSoM.
  • "Executive Council" means the Executive Council of KSoM.
  • "Rules and Regulations" means the Rules and Regulations of KSoM.
  • "Chairman" means the Chairman of the Governing Body of KSoM.
  • "Co-Chairman" means the Co-Chairman of the Governing Body of KSoM.
  • "NBHM Chairman" means the Chairman of NBHM
  • "Executive Council Chairman" means the Chairman of the Executive Council of KSoM.
  • "Competent Authority" means the authority indicated for various purposes in these rules or any other rules applicable or in force in KSoM or any such orders of the Government or Central Government issued for its Autonomous Bodies/Institutions.
  • "Director" means the Director of KSoM
  • "Registrar" means Registrar, KSoM.
  • "Finance Advisor" means the Internal financial Advisor of KSoM.

4. Aims and Objectives of KSoM

The aims and objective of KSoM are:

  • 4.1. To create and provide an atmosphere and environment suitable for creative work and pursuit of knowledge and advanced learning in the Mathematical Sciences and allied Theoretical Sciences.
  • 4.2. To promote and conduct research and original investigations in fundamental sciences in general with particular emphasis on Mathematics, Theoretical Physics, and Theoretical Computer Science.
  • 4.3. To foster a rigorous mathematical discipline, to stimulate zest for creative work and to cultivate a spirit of intellectual collaboration amongst academic workers in pure and applied branches of science.
  • 4.4. To arrange lectures, meetings, seminars, and symposia in pursuance of its academic work and for the diffusion of scientific knowledge.
  • 4.5. To invite scientists, from India and from abroad, actively engaged in creative work to deliver lectures and participate in its academic activities.
  • 4.6. To take such other steps as may be necessary and conducive to the advancement of learning in Mathematical Sciences and for the dissemination of knowledge in these sciences.
